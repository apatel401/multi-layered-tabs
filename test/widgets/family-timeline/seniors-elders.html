1971

8% of Canada’s total population are seniors (aged 65 and older), accounting for 1.8M people.

1981

9.6% of Canada’s total population are seniors (2.4M).

1985

6.8% of grandparents are aged 80 and older, a share that nearly doubles to 13.5% by 2011 (most recent data available).

1991

11.5% of Canada’s total population are seniors (3.2M), a share that continues to grow to 12.6% by 2001 (3.9M).

1991

6.7% of seniors in Canada participate in the paid labour force – rate down from 9.2% in 1976, but increases steadily to reach 14.2% by 2017.

2001

Grandparents have an average 4.8 grandchildren – declines to 4.2 by 2011.

2001

Canada is home to 3,795 centenarians (aged 100+), the first year for which this data is available.

2011

14.4% of Canada’s total population are seniors, a share that increases to 16.9% by 2017 (6.2M) and is projected to reach 23% by 2031.

2011

Canada is home to 5,825 centenarians, who become the fastest-growing age group between then and 2016 (+41%), reaching 8,230 that year.
