1971

62% of men aged 15 and older are married (breakdown of common-law rates not available until 1991).

1976

Average age at first marriage for men is 25.1 years – gradually increases to 31 years by 2008 (most recent data available).

1981

61% of men aged 15 and older are married.

1981

45% of men in their 20s are a part of a couple, a share that steadily falls to 25% by 2011.

1991

54% of men aged 15 and older are married and 7% are living common-law.

1993

56% of 11-year-old girls and 72% of boys said they found it easy to talk to their father about “things that really bother them,” a rate that increases to 66% and 75%, respectively, by 2013 (most recent data available).

2001

49% of men aged 15 and older are married and 10% are living common-law.

2001

Nearly 1 in 5 (18%) children aged 24 and under in lone-parent families live with their dad, up from 17% in 1981–1991 but lower than 22% to 23% during 1951–1971.

2011

46% of men are married, a share that increases slightly to 47% by 2017. 12% of men are living common-law, a share that declines slightly to 11% by 2017.

2011

54% of men aged 30 to 34 report never having been married (most recent data available), more than triple the share in 1981 (15%).
