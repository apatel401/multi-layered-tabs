1970

There are nearly 156,000 deaths in Canada – increases nearly every year afterward as Canada’s population grows and ages, reaching more than 259,000 by 2014.

1971

Life expectancy at birth is 76.6 years for women and 69.6 years for men – rises steadily to 83.9 years and 79.8 years, respectively, by 2012.

1980

An estimated 328 of every 100,000 deaths is due to cancer, a death rate that climbs by 21% to reach 259 per 100,000 by 2012.

1980

An estimated 741 of every 100,000 deaths is due to circulatory system diseases (e.g. heart disease, stroke), a death rate that drops by 68% to reach 236 per 100,000 by 2012.

1990

Maternal mortality rate is 7 per 100,000 live births, down significantly from 508 deaths in 1931. Rate increases to 9 deaths from 1995 to 2010, but then falls back to 7 by 2015.

1991

Infant mortality rate is 6.4 deaths per 1,000, down significantly from 76 per 1,000 in 1931 – rate falls to 4.7 by 2014.

2000

Suicide accounts for 23% of deaths among youth aged 15–24 – rate stable since then, ranging between 18% (2006) and 25% (2014).

2000

Suicide is the second-leading cause of death for this age group, with accidents consistently the leading cause (which declined from a peak of 44% in 2002 to 37% in 2014).

2012

Death rate from all causes is 580 per 100,000 women (down 61% from 1950) and 843 per 100,000 men (down 54% since 1950).

2014

62% of all deaths occur in a hospital, down from 77% in 1991.
