1976

4% of single-earner couple families with at least one child under 16 included a breadwinning mother, a rate that climbs to 21% by 2014.

1976

36% of families have two earners, a rate that climbs to 69% by 2014.

1980

Low-income rate (LIM-AT) for all households is 10.5% – increases to 14.2% by 2015.

1985

21% of married or common-law couples received “fairly equal” incomes (both partners received between 40% and 60% of the couple’s combined income) – rate climbs to 32% by 2015.

1996

3% of senior women and 10% of senior men are in the paid labour market – rates increase to 10% and 19%, respectively, by 2017.

1999

8% of families report having consumer (non-mortgage) debt that was larger than their annual after-tax family income – rate nearly doubles to 14% by 2012.

2001

Median total family income (combined total income of all family members) among all Census families is $34,100.

2008

29% of women of women in dual-earner, opposite-sex married couples earn more than their husbands, more than double the share in 1976 (12%).

2011

Median total family income (combined total income of all family members) among all Census families is $45,390 – increases to $49,840 by 2015.

2017

Average consumer (non-mortgage) debt among debtholders is nearly $22,600.
