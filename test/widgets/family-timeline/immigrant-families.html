1994

15% of immigrants are admitted as refugees – rate hovers between 9% and 17% until it jumps to 24% in 2016 (partly the result of Syrian refugee resettlement).

2001

2006

3.6% of Canada’s total population is made up of recent immigrants (arrived in the past 5 years), a rate that remains relatively stable by 2016 (3.5%).

2011

21% of the population in Canada was born outside the country, a share that climbs slightly to 22% by 2016 and is projected to grow to 25% to 30% by 2036.

2016

38% of children aged 0 to 14 are foreign-born or had at least one foreign-born parent.
